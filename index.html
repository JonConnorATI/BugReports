What Makes a Good Bug Report

I have been asked, What makes a good bug report at every interview. It is important because it helps the team fix the bug and provide metrics for the project about defects.

Opening Jira, the other day to write a ticket it hit me. Jira automatically handles the generation (or prompts for inputs) for many of the items we need to write a good report. For example, ID, Author, Severity, Priority. We shouldn’t take these for granted and understand why they are needed.

Here is a reminder, if you are preparing for interviews, what actually does make a good bug report from the ISTQB definition of a defect report.

ISTQB Definition of a Defect Report

Below are the points mentioned in the ISTQB syllabus, hover on each one, I have included a brief description.

A defect report filed during dynamic testing typically includes:

The identifier is very important in being able to refer to the defect.
An impactful bug report immediately tells anyone reading it what the problem is in the title.
The date and time that the defect occurred or reported is also essential. This is useful when you want to search for defects that were identified for a particular release of software or from when a testing phase started.
we may need to refer to the person who raised the defect, we have to know who to contact.
Developers may work with several projects concurrently, so this will inform them which application and version a bug was reproduced in.
This information shows us what phase the bug occurred in. It could help identify an issue with the integration of another module or feature.
If your bug is not reproducible, then it will never get fixed. You should clearly mention the steps to reproduce the bug. Do not assume or skip any reproducing step. A bug which is described step by step is easy to reproduce and fix.

A picture paints a thousand words so screenshots, or any outputs are desirable if available.
The difference between Actual and Expected is the bug. It allows everyone on the team to quickly understand how the bug behaves, what the bug is, how it differs from the expectation.
Bug severity is the measure of impact a defect (or bug) can have on the development or functioning of an application feature when it is being used. It depends on the effect of the bug on the system.
Bug priority refers to how urgently a bug needs to be fixed and eliminated from the website or app in question.
Informs everyone where the defect/bug is in the defect/bug lifecycle.
Mention any recommendations you have, it could help the developer.
For example the product catalogue is broken, this will effect the shopping cart.
The defect may get re opened in the future, so its good to know what steps have been taken already to resolve the issue.
Gives metrics on where bugs are appearing or which tests are revealing bugs.
